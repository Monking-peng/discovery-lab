# HelpHub research interviews

> Synthetic portfolio data. Names, companies, and events are fictional.

## Interview I-01 — Enterprise support lead

Account: Northstar Bank
Cohort: enterprise / regulated
Interviewed: 2026-06-18

“Drafting the first reply is annoying, but it rarely changes the outcome. The dangerous part is noticing that a normal-looking ticket is actually an outage or compliance issue.”

The team reviews roughly 1,800 tickets per week. Two senior agents manually scan the queue during business hours. At night, escalation depends on the assigned agent recognizing risk words and knowing the account contract.

“Last month a data-export complaint waited six hours because it was tagged as a how-to question. We breached the SLA and the customer reopened it twice.”

The lead would accept an AI suggestion if it showed the exact phrases and account rules that triggered the risk label. They would not accept automatic escalation without a human confirmation during the pilot.

## Interview I-02 — SMB support manager

Account: Willow CRM
Cohort: SMB
Interviewed: 2026-06-20

“We mostly want faster replies for password resets and billing questions. Classification is already good enough because we only have six agents.”

The manager estimates that reply drafting could save each agent twenty to thirty minutes per day. They do not have contractual severity levels and rarely operate outside business hours.

## Interview I-03 — Enterprise customer success manager

Account: Atlas Logistics
Cohort: enterprise / global
Interviewed: 2026-06-24

“Executives do not ask why the first reply took ten minutes. They ask why nobody realized a regional outage was spreading across five tickets.”

The CSM described three tickets from the same customer that were handled independently. Each looked medium priority, but together they indicated a checkout outage. The account renewed after a service-credit negotiation, but the CSM marked it as churn risk.
